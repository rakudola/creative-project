Chapter 1

You know what? You've made some dumb decisions in the past. That's a fair statement. You totaled your mom's car in high school, you almost failed out of university, you graduated with a useless degree and wandered into Taiwan to teach English even though you didn't know a single word of Mandarin. Whatever. You figured it out. Life keeps moving.

This, though? This whole situation is—

"Hey," the middle schooler in the passenger seat says, a smile across his face as he taps a finger on the gun he's got pressed to your temple. "Are you working with Tanaka or not?" Perfect English.

You're glad you're in the backseat, because Mark—the only friend from high school you've kept in touch with—looks like he's about to pass out, too. Solidarity.

"I—I didn't know what was in the bag," you stutter out, because that seems like the real issue this kid and the lady driving have with you: something's in the bag that Mr. Tien-chung (your very polite neighbor who helped you move in and brought you bread) asked you to take to his friend, and also there's a middle schooler with bright blue hair holding a gun to your head what is happening why is this happening?!

"Oh, that's nice!" He chirps. "I don't care! Answer the question."

The lady at the wheel jerks the car past the railing, and now you're all careening down a mountain in some tin can of a car, and if this kid and his gun don't kill you, the final impact probably will.

The gun leaves your head by the grace of the laws of physics alone.

Mark pulls a Ring Pop out of his pocket. He's crying.

"Will you marry me?"

"What?"

"I don't wanna die single!"

"You're engaged!!"

The car slams to a stop. The seatbelt slams into your chest, inertia knocking the wind out of you.

You barely get a second to gasp for air before the kid in the front seat has his gun trained on the both of you again, that cheerful smile still on his face.

"Get out of my car," he says cheerily. "We're stopping here for lunch!"

You unbuckle the seatbelt and get out of the car. The kid's staring Mark down like a hawk, and the lady that was driving is watching your every move. She probably has a gun on her person. You'd rather not find out.

You glance around.

Wait. You know this place.

The restaurant you're in front of is one you frequent, actually. It's tiny and run by one of the coolest guys you've ever made friends with. The guy's barely out of high school and trying to support his siblings, and, well, you probably should've known that this thing was a mafia front. Somehow. Why is this happening to you? Is this karmic retribution from a past life?

Mark sidles up to you under the careful watch of whatever triad members just herded you out of their car.

"What's the weather like in Springville?" You ask. You're not sure why. Maybe you're just homesick and also acutely aware that you probably won't see your apartment again, much less your hometown.

"54 Fahrenheit, light rain," he says, and you know he hasn't had an opportunity to check recently. How does he do it?

You march into the restaurant to Chia-wei's standard giant grin and "Hey, good to see you. What can I—"

And then he sees the psychopaths trail in behind you.

"—get for you," he finishes. You aren't great at subtler hints in this language yet, but it's not hard to tell he's less than pleased.

"Oh, the usual," Mr. Middle Schooler says pleasantly, easily slipping into Mandarin.

Chia-wei points at you and Mark, eyes still glued to the kid with blue hair. "Those two aren't in on anything. They can barely find their way around town. What's wrong with you?"

The lady who was driving is behind you, so you don't see so much as feel the death glare she sends at Chia-wei. The temperature drops a few degrees. You're not sure if you're glad that the place is empty or not.

"I would hardly call moving drugs for Tanaka 'not in on anything,'" the kid shoots back. "Do you still serve youtiao?"

"Of course. Stop messing with the foreigners and I'll give you extra."

"Only if they tell me what I want to know!"

Chia-wei's dropped his smile by this point, opting for a face loudly signaling a migraine. "And you've gotten a whole lot of useful information out of them already, right?"

"Hm... I know what the weather is in Springville!" Another big smile.

"Look, Haruki, they probably just thought they were doing a favor." Chia-wei's pinching his nose bridge. "If you want actual information, you've just let Tanaka know you're after him, and now he's probably on the next flight to Korea."

Haruki pouts thoughtfully. "Hm. Yeah. That makes sense."

Chia-wei pulls his fingers away from his face, mild confusion painted on it. "What, you're not gonna argue?"

Haruki shrugs. "We've been on this for three months, and I'm running out of vacation days. I really just want this entire thing over and done with."

"And it didn't occur to you that these two were probably in contact with Tanaka before you stuffed them in your car at gunpoint?"

"I've slept a grand total of 12 hours this past week," the woman finally says. "Can we get our food and leave?"

"Please do," Chia-wei says.

She rattles off her and Haruki's orders. Chia-wei gives her the minimum needed attention before turning to you and Mark, exhausted but friendly smile back in place.

"Mark, Cam, what can I get you two? And don't worry about paying." He jerks a thumb at the actual mafia members that he just lectured. "They can cover it."

"Yeah! It's on me," the middle schooler, in a uniform you easily recognize as the one three blocks from your apartment complex, says, pulling one of those insane VIP Golden Luxe Exclusive credit cards out of who-know-where like he wasn't holding a gun to your head seven minutes ago.

"Uh," you say, something vaguely resembling etiquette trying to kick in. "You—You don't have to—"

"I'm not Taiwanese. Don't play that etiquette game with me." He slides the card towards Chia-wei. "It's an apology, anyway."

Chia-wei pretty much hovers over you and Mark for the rest of the meal, chatting about whatever, until Haruki and the lady whose name you don't know start packing up to leave.

Well, Chia-wei still stands there, because Haruki walks up to you with something in his hands.

"So, um. Sorry for kidnapping you," he says too brightly for the words that just came out of his mouth. "Here's my business card. If you need anything, we'll find a way to make it up to you."

He holds out the card with both hands like exchanging business cards is a thing middle schoolers do. Or maybe they do. You haven't been in middle school for a while. Maybe this is the new Pokemon.

You take the card, anyway. It's entirely in Japanese, but you're pretty sure you see the word for "hospital" on it.

"Anyway, we need to go hunt down the guy that tried to get you into drug trafficking, and our flight leaves in a few hours. See you." He waves and wanders out with the lady.

The room fades out to silence once they're out the door, save Mark slurping his noodles like he's going to starve if he doesn't.

"So," Chia-wei says slowly. "How did you like your brush with the yakuza?"

"I vote we never do that again," Mark says around his noodles. "Anyway, fun fact: Springville's at 32 percent humidity right now."

"Is it," you mumble, wondering how much money it would cost to bury today's events in dumplings.

The End (For Now)
